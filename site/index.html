I got my first camera when I was 10 years old. My mother did not like the cost of printing photos, thus would grawl at me. I wish I grew up with digital photography.

I spent a summer at NESOP (New England School of Photography) in Boston and then did two independent studies at Connecticut College, in New London. I spent hours developing negatives, checking contact sheets and printing. I loved the photo enravelling just in front of me.

My interests in photography have covered the very small electron microscopy to what is observable with our naked eyes.

I have been mesmerized by Nature perhaps because I grew up in Africa or because of my training in biology. But beauty and the unbelievable structures and functions in nature are simply splendid.

Anthropology opened my eyes to Humans in their environments and their interactions with the group and nature.

Nature and Humans have been at the center of every photo I have take. Some are more conceptual than others but all captured a moment.
